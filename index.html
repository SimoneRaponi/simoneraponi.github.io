Simone Raponi

AI & Cybersecurity  ·  From the Dark Web to the Deep Sea

"From the Dark Web to the Deep Sea" is the literal arc of my career, a decade of bringing Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning to bear on adversarial problems at the extremes of the digital and physical world. It began underground: hunting anonymous actors in criminal networks, exposing their behavioral fingerprints, and mapping their geographic origins through layers of deliberate obfuscation. From there, the scope expanded across communication protocols, critical infrastructures, the electromagnetic spectrum, and the maritime domain, always guided by the same question: where can intelligent systems make the decisive difference between an attacker succeeding and failing? Today, that question has carried me to the ocean floor.

I am currently Senior Technical Team Leader at WSENSE, where I lead the Artificial Intelligence, the Computer Vision, and the Cybersecurity divisions across several active subsea projects: vessel classification via hydrophones, near real-time fish detection and tracking, biomass estimation, automated pellet counting, low-bandwidth acoustic video streaming, subsea digital twins through 3D/4D reconstruction, and AI chatbots for interpreting complex telemetry data. I also serve as Designated Officer for Classified Data and Information Security, and as Senior Technical Project Manager, overseeing NATO Restricted defense projects, National Military Research Plans, and Defense-based Integrated Underwater Systems.

Before WSENSE, I co-founded and led the AI Division at Equixly as the company's 4th employee. I personally designed and built the core API security scanning engine from scratch, including the full endpoint-exploration orchestrator, advanced parameter-resolution strategies, and inter-parameter dependency computation. The result: scan times reduced by 70% vs. state-of-the-art, vulnerability detection accuracy improved by 80% vs. market competitors, and a direct contribution to closing a €10M Series A round.

At NATO CMRE, I drove the ML workstream of the PROMENADE European project, designing Encoder-Decoder Seq2Seq models with LSTMs for maritime vessel trajectory forecasting using AIS and raw radar data. My models reduced prediction error to 2.5 nautical miles over 9-hour horizons, 70% lower than existing solutions.

My research sits at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and Cybersecurity, spanning an unusually wide range of adversarial frontiers: unmasking and geolocating anonymous actors in the Dark Web through behavioral time-zone profiling and native-language fingerprinting of forum crowds; authenticating LEO satellite transmitters at the physical layer by training Convolutional Neural Networks on over 100 million IQ samples from the IRIDIUM constellation; detecting cryptojacking at the network layer, even across encrypted VPN tunnels, by fingerprinting the traffic signatures of Bitcoin, Monero, and Bytecoin mining operations; classifying firearms by category, caliber, and model from muzzle-blast audio recordings with zero knowledge of the recording setup; detecting keystrokes on wireless keyboards at up to 15 meters through pure RF emission analysis, independent of encryption scheme or physical obstacles; designing crypto-less key-establishment protocols for IoT devices through frequency-hopping radio collisions; monitoring critical undersea infrastructures, from the Nord Stream gas pipelines to submarine communication cables, via seabed-to-space AI-driven sensor fusion; dissecting the systemic vulnerabilities of software supply chains in the wake of the SolarWinds attack; auditing the Docker container ecosystem for architectural security flaws; securing vessel communications and autonomous maritime navigation; and modeling disinformation as a precision weapon of modern information warfare.

I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from Hamad Bin Khalifa University, Qatar, awarded both the Best Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering Award and the Outstanding Performance Award. My B.Sc. and M.Sc. in Computer Science, both earned at Sapienza University of Rome, were awarded 110/110 summa cum laude.
